A NASA-collaboration project to turn the **Rice Convection Model (RCM)** into a real-time nowcast tool for **injections of energetic particles into geosynchronous orbit** during substorms.

### What we built
- A streaming RCM driver that consumes solar-wind and IMF data and produces minute-cadence inner-magnetosphere plasma-flow and field-aligned-current fields.
- An injection-detection pipeline that flags candidate substorm-driven energetic-particle injection events at GEO and characterizes their azimuthal extent and duration.
- An analysis framework that benchmarks the operational RCM forecasts against in-situ GOES &amp; LANL observations.

### Talks
- *Towards a Nowcast Model of Injections into Geosynchronous Orbit* &mdash; AGU Fall Meeting 2022, Chicago.
- *Development of Operational RCM Project* &mdash; GEM (Geospace Environment Modeling) Workshop 2022.

This was my first major project at Rice and remains the foundation for the more detailed dipolarization &amp; bubble work that became my dissertation (see *OpOF / BackTrac*).
