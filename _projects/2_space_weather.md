Space weather — the chain of energetic phenomena driven by the Sun and felt at Earth — affects satellites, communications, GPS, and the power grid. Operational nowcasting connects scientific models of the magnetosphere to real-time data streams so that warnings can be issued before particles or fields impact infrastructure.

Within this area I work on the following projects:

- **Operational RCM with NASA.** A streaming pipeline that drives the Rice Convection Model (RCM) with minute-cadence solar-wind and IMF data and flags candidate substorm injections of energetic particles at geosynchronous orbit, with their azimuthal extent, duration, and an expected Dst signature; benchmarked against in-situ GOES and LANL observations.
- **Substorm injection physics.** Connecting the operational forecasts to the underlying physics of plasma-sheet flow channels, dipolarization fronts, and the transition from open- to closed-field-line topology — providing the physical motivation behind the empirical predictors a nowcasting service relies on.
