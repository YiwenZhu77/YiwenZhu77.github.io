A NASA-collaboration project that turned the **Rice Convection Model (RCM)** into a streaming, real-time nowcasting tool for **substorm injections of energetic particles into geosynchronous orbit (GEO)**. The system ingests minute-cadence solar-wind and IMF data, drives RCM through the inner magnetosphere, and flags candidate injection events with their azimuthal extent, duration, and an expected Dst signature.

Forecast accuracy was benchmarked against in-situ GOES and LANL particle observations across multiple sawtooth and isolated-substorm intervals. This was my first major project at Rice (AGU 2022, GEM 2022) and the foundation for the more detailed dipolarization &amp; bubble work that became my dissertation.
